Energy production and consumption


Energy deals represent about 50% of all traded weather derivatives. In France the variations in temperature account for 85% of the variance in winter energy demand and 63% in summer. The negative anomaly of the air temperature in winter triggers the additional volume demand of the natural gas and the electricity around 3% per 1 degree C. Few examples of financial risks related to the air temperature variations.


Depending on the weather anomaly and the power load, the planning of generators is re-adjusted. Cold anomaly of 1 degree C implies a greater demand (better sales), but also requires the additional boost of the coal, natural gas and oil power plants, which is costly at the last moment.


To fulfill the volume engagements, the wind farm energy provider has to buy the expensive wholesale electricity simply because the weak or unstable winds unable it's own production. Weather derivatives replenish the profit losses proportionally to the anomaly duration and intensity.

Agricultural Commodities


Persistent and repetitive bad weather conditions dictate the yield volumes and quality, the production costs and as a result the commodity prices. Moreover, an excessive soil moisture triggers a higher exposure of plants to diseases and insects, which increases the demand in fertilisers and chemicals, affecting their stocks and prices. The transport planning and as a result, the production cost, is also reconsidered following a regional failure in yield.


To support farmers when coping with the concequences of unfavorable non-extreme weather, the weather-derivatives contracts become useful. These formalise the frequency and the intencity of unwelcomed events. For example, this could be the short repetitive droughts over the season, when no rainfall but high air temperatures. In elevated and continental regions frost events are of concern. The anomalously long/cold winter is the most commonly traded event on agricultural commodity market.

Temperature-based HDD-index reflects well the unusually cold months and seasons. Another danger in the fields are the storm-force winds and heavy showers, excluded from a classic insuarance contract. Cattle and poultry producers suffer from droughts by means of the feed cost, forced to buy the grains elsewhere. The list of primary goods requiring the weather-based hedging is as long as the variety of agricultural spicies. The top 10 include the oilseeds, cocoa, corn, wheat, soya, sugar cane, rice, coffee, cotton, potatoes and fruits.

Air Temperature


The operational real-time weather forecasts reflect quite well the evolution in air temperature and wind over coming 3-4 days. Yet, the complexity of meteorological events does not allow for a longer thermo-dynamic (physical) weather forecast. You might have heard about the seasonal forecasts. These are built from historical 40-year weather records and the regional tendencies in the Sea Level Pressure. Indeed, the seasonal forecasts reflect mostly some statistically based expectation rather than a real-time thermo-dynamic weather forecast.
So far, beyond one week ahead, the future financial losses due to unpredictable weather anomaly are unpredictable as well. As a result, from day-to-day, from season-to-season the temperature anomaly increases the gap between the expected revenue and the actual one.



Air temperature is the most popular and a highly reliable meteorological parameter. Observations are available all over the Globe. For financial applications the daily average values are recalculated into indexes such as the cumulative Heating (HDD) and the cumulative Cooling Degree Days (CDD), the Heat Index (HI), the Cumulative Average Temperature. The cumulative term means that the daily air temperature anomaly is summed over a period, which can be a week, month or season. Around 80% of contracts are based on either temperature index. According to a swap-type contract, one counter party gets paid if the degree days over a specified period are greater than the strike level, and the other party gets paid if the degree days over that period are less than the strike.

Solar radiation



Numerical weather models and observations allow for daily estimates of the incoming solar radiation intensity. This parameter determines the performance of the photovoltaic power plants, and explains well certain commercial activities and sales volumes. Solar radiation can be represented in terms of the :
Sunshine duration [hours per day],
Solar illuminance index [% of maximum available],
Anomaly of the solar radiation intensity [Watt/m2]


Additionally, many other meteorological parameters are available for any site worldwide. These are, for example, the soil moisture (satellite data), the evaporation intensity, the air quality (measurements), ocean wave height (satellite data), the presence of snow, the sea ice concentrations and ice drift velocity.
